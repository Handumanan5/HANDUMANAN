Sunugin Natin Ang Ating Mga Paa

Interpretations, Symbolisms and Meanings

We took inspiration from the story we have read about the two friends who were stuck in a windmill, as the windmill as caught up in fire. 

This assemblage showcases a bittersweet story of love and destruction. It captures the essence of love amidst turmoil—a sense of both beauty and tragedy in their connection.

The two lovers, represented by stone, are caught in the midst of a fire as symbolized by the leaves. As they gaze into each other 's eyes, there 's a sense of vulnerability and passion; their hearts entwined amidst the chaos of their imminent demise.

The wires, resembling smoke, further emphasize the intensity of their situation. It serve as a haunting reminder of the fragility of their existence, reminding us of our own mortality and how life can be taken from us at any time, and the fleeting nature of their love as well. 

The branches entangling them, additionally, could signify the entrapment or inevitability of their fate. They are reminded that they can never escape the fire, and all that they can do is to accept their fate and their impending death. 

The title "Sunugin Natin ang Ating Mga Paa” adds another layer of depth to the assemblage 's bittersweet narrative. The imagery of burning feet suggests a willingness to endure pain and sacrifice for the sake of love. 

It implies a sense of shared suffering and solidarity between the lovers, as they confront the fire together, even if it means their own death. 

Moreover, the act of burning one 's feet symbolizes a commitment to journeying through hardship and adversity hand in hand, emphasizing the strength of their relationship.

The title perfectly serves to reinforce the emotional intensity and selflessness depicted in our artwork

From objects to stories

A closer look into our life 's everyday pieces turned into a masterpiece.

the making.

Before making the assemblage, we first walked throughout the whole school in search of materials that could be used for the artwork. We got a lot of useful things such as bricks, a bunch of rocks, leaves, sticks, a plastic rope, and a bendable metal wire. We were given a total of twenty minutes to gather our materials. After that, we sat down and discussed ideas on what to work on using the various materials. The brainstorming took a bit of time but we finally decided on an idea proposed by one of our group mates which was based on a story that happened in real life. There were a bit of difficulties during the making of the assemblage since we didn 't really have the best of materials but we made do with what we had.
