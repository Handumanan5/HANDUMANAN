SCULPTURE

“Where’s the man who threw blanket over my barbed wires?”

“Where’s the man who threw blanket over my barbed wires?”

This sculpture portrays the hand of a wife who has been admiring her husband for so long, but now she’s starting to show her cracks in admiration; she’s growing so tired and desperate that she just wants him to admit to her that the relationship is so broken to break her out of the fantasy that she is stuck in. Some part of her knows that she deserves better but she’s so reliant on him and so encompassed by him that she can’t leave him. She worships him. She excuses and brushes off his carelessness and poor judgment. She sits by and takes the backseat to him. She does all she can to appease him and gain his validation because of his hold on her. The barbed wire in the sculpture represents the entanglement of the wife's emotions and the complexities of her relationship with her husband. Each barb symbolizes a moment of pain, disappointment, and disillusionment that she has endured while clinging to the fantasy of their relationship. The wire is tightly wound around her hand, illustrating the grip that her husband has on her, both emotionally and psychologically. Despite the sharpness of the wire digging into her skin, she continues to hold on, unable to break free from the illusion of their connection. The flowers in the sculpture serve as a metaphor for the wife's hopes, dreams, and desires within the relationship. Each blossom represents a tender moment, a cherished memory, or a heartfelt wish that she holds dear. The vibrant colors and delicate petals symbolize the beauty and joy that she finds in her love for her husband. However, the presence of wilted flowers amidst the blossoming flowers signifies the gradual disillusionment and fading of her romantic ideals. Just as flowers require nurturing and care to thrive, so too does their relationship, but neglect and indifference have taken their toll. This juxtaposition of vibrant blooms with withered ones reflects the complexities of love and the inevitable passage of time.

On the day of this sculpting activity, half of the group was called up to partake in their respective club activities. Rightfully so, the remaining 4 members were left with the creative responsibility of conceptualizing the sculpture. After a brief brainstorming session, the group decided on a sculpture that evoked strong feelings of grief and anguish. This was evident in the gritty carved areas and the chosen forms—flowers and barbed wire—which conveyed a pervasive sense of longing, emphasizing a sense of affect and expression. Sculpting tasks were frugally delegated considering the lack of available members during the process, each member had a specific job in the sculpting; the sculpting began immediately after the conception of the main idea to save as much time as possible to give room for any alterations. New ideas on the symbolisms and the artistic narrative soon became clearer to the members as the sculpting proceeded in its stages which finally, led to the unique naming of the piece “Where’s the man who threw blankets over my barbed wires”.
